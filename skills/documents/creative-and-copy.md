# Creative writing and copywriting

## Overview
Two related crafts: **creative** (fiction, scripts, narrative) and **copy** (persuasive
writing that drives an action). Both live or die on voice and specificity.

## When to use
"Write a short story about…", "write a script/scene for…", "write landing-page copy for…",
"give me a slogan / headline / ad for…", "make this sound more compelling".

## Workflow
1. For creative: establish premise, POV, tone, and length. For copy: establish the offer,
   audience, the one action you want, and the platform/format.
2. Creative — open with a concrete image or tension, not exposition; show don't tell; end
   with intent. Copy — lead with the reader's problem or desire, one clear value prop, one
   call to action; cut every word that doesn't earn its place.
3. Offer 2–3 variants for short copy (headlines, slogans, hooks).
4. Save to `documents/<slug>.md`.

## Types covered
Short story · flash fiction · scene/screenplay · spoken-word/poem · landing-page copy ·
ad copy · slogans/taglines · email/sales copy · product descriptions · brand voice notes.

## Quality bar
Creative: a distinct voice and a real arc, no clichés or filler. Copy: specific, benefit-
led, one CTA, no hype words that say nothing; honest claims only.

## Examples
- "Write a 300-word story about a lighthouse keeper." → atmospheric, single arc.
- "Landing-page copy for a 6-week build cohort." → hook + value + proof + one CTA, plus
  3 headline variants.
